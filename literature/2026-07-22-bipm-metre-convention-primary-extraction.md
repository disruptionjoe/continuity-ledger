## Source-grounded extraction

| Result | Source location | Boundary |
|---|---|---|
| The Convention establishes a permanent international bureau and a General Conference/International Committee structure for common action on units. | Metre Convention, arts. 1-3; BIPM official-text introduction | Institutional organization, not a physical conservation law. |
| The Bureau's duties include prototype verification, periodic comparison of national and international standards, and calibration/comparison of precision and geodetic standards. | Annexed Regulations, arts. 6-7 | Comparison and calibration are explicit operations with institutional maintenance costs. |
| SI units are defined through seven defining constants; the metre fixes the numerical value of `c` at 299 792 458 m s^-1. | SI Brochure sections 1.1 and 2.2; BIPM metre page | Typed unit definitions do not collapse physical quantities into one scalar. |
| SI definitions are the highest reference level for traceability; metrology institutes realize them, and Consultative Committees establish equivalence of realizations worldwide. | SI Brochure section 1.3, p. 123 | Traceability is an organized relation from realizations to definitions, not identity between measurements. |
| Primary realization methods use equations of physics, and multiple realizations may improve as technology advances. | SI Brochure section 2.3.2, pp. 132-133 | The definition/realization distinction is native metrology vocabulary and already explains the flexibility. |

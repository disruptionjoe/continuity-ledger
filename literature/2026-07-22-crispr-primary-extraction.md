# CRISPR Spacer Acquisition Primary Extraction

## Scope and source

Question: does CRISPR spacer acquisition require an explanation beyond
sequence-specific prokaryotic adaptive immunity?

Primary source: Barrangou et al., "CRISPR Provides Acquired Resistance Against
Viruses in Prokaryotes," *Science* 315 (2007), 1709-1712,
https://doi.org/10.1126/science.1138140; bibliographic record and abstract at
https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17379808/. This read is isolated from CL-001
and makes no cross-case synthesis.

## Extraction

- After viral challenge, bacteria integrated new spacers derived from phage
  genomic sequences.
- Adding or removing particular spacers changed the phage-resistance phenotype.
- Resistance specificity depended on similarity between spacer and phage
  sequence, with CRISPR and associated `cas` genes providing the mechanism.
- The result establishes a causal sequence-specific defense phenotype. It does
  not establish unrestricted novelty, escape from genomic possibility space,
  or a general agency-generating transition.
- The relevant imports include prior CRISPR-Cas machinery, exposure to phage,
  genomic integration, expression, and sequence matching.

## Absorber verdict

`NARROWER_STRUCTURE_SEQUENCE_SPECIFIC_ADAPTIVE_IMMUNITY`.

The primary result is explained by molecular acquisition and sequence-specific
immune defense. A new spacer changes future resistance, but the paper does not
force an independent transduction law, a new agent class, or recursive
capability growth. No CL-001 field or shared atlas claim moves.

## Handoff

Retain CRISPR as an adaptive-immunity absorber control. Reopen only if evidence
shows a residual beyond phage exposure, spacer integration, CRISPR-Cas
machinery, expression, and sequence-specific recognition.

# Pacioli Bookkeeping Treatise Extraction

## Scope and source

Question: does Pacioli's bookkeeping system require an explanation beyond
ordinary accounting notation and posting discipline?

The Library of Congress *History of Accounting* guide identifies Pacioli's
bookkeeping treatise within the 1494 *Summa* and routes readers to reproduced
Italian text and English translations:
https://guides.loc.gov/history-of-accounting/practice/early-history. This
bounded read uses John B. Geijsbeek's 1914 public-domain reproduction and
translation, *Ancient Double-Entry Bookkeeping*. Translation risk is retained;
modern terminology is not treated as a verbatim 1494 formula. The read is
isolated from CL-001.

## Extraction

- The treatise prescribes an inventory followed by memorandum, journal, and
  ledger books, with dated entries and cross-references.
- Journal entries explain transactions before ledger posting; ledger pages
  separate debtor and creditor treatment and carry page references for audit.
- Monetary valuation makes heterogeneous goods postable in a common account
  unit, but does not equate goods, cash, profit, and ownership.
- Closing and balancing procedures expose omissions or posting discrepancies.
  Their force comes from maintained convention and documents, not a physical
  conservation law.
- The system imports literacy, valuation judgment, clerical labor, books,
  documentary retention, and disciplined repeated posting.

## Absorber verdict

`NARROWER_STRUCTURE_ACCOUNTING_POSTING_DISCIPLINE`.

The transition is explained by documentary workflow, valuation,
journal-to-ledger translation, paired posting, cross-reference, and balance
checks. These support audit and coordination but do not prove a newly recruited
agent class, independent transduction law, or recursive capability growth. No
CL-001 field or shared atlas claim moves.

# Lac Operon Primary Extraction

## Scope and source

Question: does lac regulation require an explanation beyond ordinary genetic
regulatory control?

Primary source: F. Jacob and J. Monod, "Genetic Regulatory Mechanisms in the
Synthesis of Proteins," *Journal of Molecular Biology* 3 (1961), 318-356,
https://doi.org/10.1016/S0022-2836(61)80072-7; bibliographic record at
https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/13718526/. This read is isolated from CL-001
and makes no cross-case synthesis.

## Extraction

- The paper distinguishes structural genes from regulatory elements and models
  coordinated protein synthesis through an operon-level control system.
- A regulator product acts at an operator associated with the structural genes;
  inducer conditions change repression and therefore enzyme synthesis.
- The regulated output depends on inherited gene organization, molecular
  recognition, transcriptional machinery, substrates, and cellular energy.
- Conditional enzyme production changes metabolic behavior, but does not create
  a new organismal agent, an unbounded action space, or escape from the genome's
  regulatory possibility class.
- The source's messenger and operator model is a mechanistic proposal grounded
  in the experiments available in 1961; later molecular detail must not be
  silently attributed to this paper.

## Absorber verdict

`NARROWER_STRUCTURE_GENE_REGULATORY_CONTROL`.

The source-grounded transition is explained by regulatory genes, operator
control, induction, messenger-mediated expression, and existing metabolic
machinery. No independent transduction law or newly recruited agent class is
forced. No CL-001 field or shared atlas claim moves.

## Handoff

Retain the lac operon as a gene-regulation absorber control. Reopen only for a
specific primary-source dispute or evidence of a residual beyond inherited
regulatory organization, inducer/repressor interaction, transcription, and
metabolic machinery.

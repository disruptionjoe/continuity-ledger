# Torrens Title Primary Extraction

## Scope and sources

Question: does Torrens title require an explanation beyond statutory property
law and registry administration?

Authoritative primary sources:

- South Australia, *Real Property Act No. 16 of 22 Vic, 1858*, official
  historical numbered-act scan, linked from
  https://www.legislation.sa.gov.au/lists-and-index-to-sa-legislation/historical-numbered-acts-1837-2002/1851-1860
- South Australia, *Real Property Act 1886*, official legislation page:
  https://www.legislation.sa.gov.au/lz?path=%2Fc%2Fa%2Freal+property+act+1886

The 1858 scan fixes the seed's historical statute identity. The continuing Act
is used only to bound the operative registration structure. This read is
isolated from CL-001 and makes no cross-case synthesis.

## Extraction

- The statutory system creates a government Register Book and a
  Registrar-General, with procedures for bringing land under the Act,
  publishing notice, and receiving caveats.
- Registration is constitutive within the scheme: section 67 provides that an
  instrument does not pass land or make it security until registration.
- The registered proprietor's title prevails subject to the Act's stated
  encumbrances, interests, and exceptions; the legal effect is therefore typed
  and exception-bounded, not universal irreversibility.
- Priority, memorials, retained records, certificates, searches, caveats, and
  court-directed correction are institutional machinery and imported costs.
- The register recognizes legal estates and interests. It does not equate
  legal title with survey geometry, market price, possession, moral legitimacy,
  or the entire social history of the land.

## Primary-Source Finding

Nealson, Platt, and Hastings, "Cellular Control of the Synthesis and Activity
of the Bacterial Luminescent System," *Journal of Bacteriology* 104 (1970),
313-322, DOI `10.1128/jb.104.1.313-322.1970`, is available from
https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC248216/ and indexed at
https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/5473898/.

The paper reports that luciferase synthesis is inactive in a freshly inoculated
culture and later occurs in a short burst during exponential growth. The
authors attribute transcriptional activation to conditioning of the medium by
growing cells. Minimal-medium cultures retain the pattern at lower magnitude
relative to cell mass. The bounded source therefore supports a population- and
medium-conditioned regulatory response; it does not by itself establish the
later full molecular vocabulary of quorum sensing.

Discovery disposition:
`NARROWER_STRUCTURE_DENSITY_CONDITIONED_GENE_REGULATION`.

The source-grounded behavior is absorbed by secreted-medium conditioning,
population growth, transcriptional activation, existing luciferase machinery,
and metabolic resources. It does not force a new bacterial agent class,
unbounded action space, or independent transduction law. This is an
exploration-grade local finding only; no CL-001 field or atlas synthesis moves.

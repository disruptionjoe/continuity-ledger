## Primary-Source Finding

Blount, Borland, and Lenski, "Historical Contingency and the Evolution of a Key
Innovation in an Experimental Population of Escherichia coli," *PNAS* 105
(2008), 7899-7906, DOI `10.1073/pnas.0803151105`, is available at
https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC2430337/ and indexed as PubMed
`18524956`.

Twelve populations began from the same ancestor in glucose-limited medium with
abundant citrate. Aerobic citrate use did not appear for more than 30,000
generations, then arose in one population by generation 31,500. Frozen-fossil
replay experiments showed that later ancestors in that lineage had an elevated
propensity to evolve the Cit+ phenotype, supporting historical potentiation
rather than a single uniformly available mutation. Cit+ increased population
size and supported ecological diversity.

Discovery disposition:
`NARROWER_STRUCTURE_CONTINGENT_EVOLUTIONARY_INNOVATION`.

The source establishes a real new metabolic phenotype and a history-dependent
change in mutational accessibility. It is explained within experimental
evolution by inherited genotype, mutation, potentiating history, selection,
resource opportunity, and ecological feedback. The paper does not establish
escape from biological possibility space, a new agent class, or an independent
transduction law. This local finding moves no CL-001 field or atlas synthesis.

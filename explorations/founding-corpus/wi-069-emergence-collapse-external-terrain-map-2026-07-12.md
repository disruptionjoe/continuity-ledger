# External Terrain Map: Emergence, Collapse, Obstruction, Escape Accounting

Status: staged exploration file
Date: 2026-07-12
Scope: external literature and domain terrain scan before running the pre-repo experiment

## Purpose

This file maps existing similar and tangential work that could absorb, pressure, or strengthen the proposed emergence/collapse accounting program.

The goal is not to collect references. The goal is to discover whether the proposed research object is already owned elsewhere, whether it can be made rigorous, and which domain-specific advances may generalize.

Working candidate to pressure:

> A class-relative obstruction calculus for open-ended systems that tracks how local admissibility, attempted global completion, no-go obstruction, boundary import, escape move, and surviving residue transform across domains.

## High-Level Read

The broad terrain already contains strong pieces:

- open-ended evolution studies novelty, stepping stones, quality diversity, and generated environments;
- applied sheaf theory studies local-to-global consistency and obstruction;
- distributed systems gives sharp no-go/escape frontiers for coordination, consistency, and availability;
- ecology and resilience theory studies basins, thresholds, adaptive cycles, and transformability;
- model collapse gives a clean recursive feedback collapse fixture;
- commons governance studies anti-capture and durable institutional self-governance;
- supply-chain viability studies survivability rather than return-to-baseline;
- physiology studies stability through change and overload;
- language evolution studies transmission bottlenecks and emergent structure;
- assembly theory and algorithmic complexity study construction histories, selection, and measurable complexity.

The gap is not that nobody studies emergence or collapse.

The possible gap is:

> No existing field seems to provide a reusable accounting object that simultaneously records local validity, attempted globalization, class no-go, absorber, escape burden, boundary import, and surviving residue across divergent domains.

That is the strongest case for a pre-repo experiment.

## Terrain Cluster 1: Open-Ended Evolution, Novelty Search, QD, POET

### Core Work

Open-ended evolution (OEE), novelty search, quality-diversity algorithms, MAP-Elites, POET, and Enhanced POET directly address continued novelty generation, deceptive objectives, archives of diversity, generated environments, and stepping-stone transfer.

Representative sources:

- Lehman and Stanley, "Abandoning Objectives: Evolution Through the Search for Novelty Alone": https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20868264/
- Lehman and Stanley PDF: https://www.cs.swarthmore.edu/~meeden/DevelopmentalRobotics/lehman_ecj11.pdf
- Why Open-Endedness Matters: https://direct.mit.edu/artl/article/25/3/232/2917/Why-Open-Endedness-Matters
- POET: https://arxiv.org/abs/1901.01753
- Enhanced POET PDF: https://proceedings.mlr.press/v119/wang20l/wang20l.pdf
- Quality Diversity overview: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/robotics-and-ai/articles/10.3389/frobt.2016.00040/full

### What It Already Owns

- Novelty versus objective optimization.
- Deceptive gradients as collapse into local objective traps.
- Archives as diversity-preserving memory.
- Environment-agent co-generation.
- Transfer as a source of emergent stepping stones.

### Generalizable Primitive

`Archive + emitter + novelty metric + transfer operator + environment generator`

This is close to an operational "open-endedness engine."

### Absorber Risk For Our Program

The proposed repo must not simply rebrand OEE. OEE already owns many computational novelty and diversity mechanisms.

### What Our Program Could Add

OEE usually does not packetize:

- the completion class being escaped;
- the no-go theorem or absorber being avoided;
- hidden boundary imports;
- local-to-global obstruction;
- finality or legitimacy collapse;
- whether novelty changes source admissibility or just readout.

### Research Implication

Use OEE as a positive witness family, not as the repo's owner. The proposed accounting should ask: when does novelty search become observer-side novelty, and when does it generate new admissibility or construction rules?

## Terrain Cluster 2: Model Collapse And Recursive Synthetic Data

### Core Work

Model collapse is one of the cleanest modern collapse fixtures: recursive training on generated data can remove tails of the original distribution and degrade the generative model.

Representative sources:

- Nature, "AI models collapse when trained on recursively generated data": https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-024-07566-y
- arXiv, "The Curse of Recursion": https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.17493
- "Self-Consuming Generative Models Go MAD": https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.01850
- "Is Model Collapse Inevitable?": https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.01413

### What It Already Owns

- Recursive feedback collapse.
- Loss of distribution tails.
- Synthetic data loops as source starvation.
- Preservation of original data or accumulating real data as anti-collapse input.

### Generalizable Primitive

`Boundary-source preservation`

Collapse occurs when a process consumes its own outputs as if they were independent source data.

### Absorber Risk For Our Program

This could absorb any "collapse via source exhaustion" hypothesis in the ML domain.

### What Our Program Could Add

Model collapse is an ideal `BoundaryImportPacket` fixture:

```text
apparent loop: model -> generated data -> model
hidden import: independent real data distribution
collapse: tail loss and narrowing
escape: preserve or accumulate external source data
residue: distributional diversity not reproduced by the loop
```

### Research Implication

Treat model collapse as the first executable domain-specific test of boundary import accounting.

## Terrain Cluster 3: Sheaf Theory, Contextuality, And Local-To-Global Obstruction

### Core Work

Sheaf theory and contextuality provide a precise local-to-global obstruction template: local sections can be mutually compatible in restricted contexts while no global section exists.

Representative sources:

- Abramsky and Brandenburger, "The Sheaf-Theoretic Structure of Non-Locality and Contextuality": https://arxiv.org/abs/1102.0264
- Abramsky, Mansfield, Barbosa, "The Cohomology of Non-Locality and Contextuality": https://arxiv.org/abs/1111.3620
- Contextuality, Cohomology and Paradox: https://drops.dagstuhl.de/storage/00lipics/lipics-vol041-csl2015/LIPIcs.CSL.2015.211/LIPIcs.CSL.2015.211.pdf

### What It Already Owns

- Local consistency.
- Global obstruction.
- Cohomological witnesses.
- Hierarchies of no-go strength.
- Contextuality as no global section.

### Generalizable Primitive

`Local sections + restriction maps + gluing condition + obstruction class`

### Absorber Risk For Our Program

If the proposed obstruction calculus is only "use sheaves everywhere," the new repo is not justified.

### What Our Program Could Add

The proposed program asks a different question:

- Does the obstruction merely show no global section?
- Or does it force a productive escape, class exit, boundary import, observer shift, or new admissibility rule?

This requires accounting beyond the obstruction class itself.

### Research Implication

Sheaf/contextuality machinery should be a mathematical donor, not the repo's identity.

## Terrain Cluster 5: Distributed Systems No-Gos And Positive Frontiers

### Core Work

Distributed systems has unusually clear class-relative no-go theorems and escape/frontier results.

Representative sources:

- FLP impossibility: https://groups.csail.mit.edu/tds/papers/Lynch/jacm85.pdf
- CAP theorem perspectives: https://groups.csail.mit.edu/tds/papers/Gilbert/Brewer2.pdf
- CALM theorem: https://arxiv.org/abs/1901.01930
- Coordination avoidance / invariant confluence: https://arxiv.org/abs/1402.2237
- VLDB version: https://www.vldb.org/pvldb/vol8/p185-bailis.pdf

### What It Already Owns

- Impossibility under explicitly named system assumptions.
- Escape by modifying timing, failure model, probability, coordination, invariants, or consistency goal.
- Positive classification of when coordination is unnecessary.
- Monotonicity as a boundary between coordination-free and coordination-required programs.

### Generalizable Primitive

`Class-relative no-go + assumption exit + positive frontier`

### Absorber Risk For Our Program

Distributed systems already has excellent no-go accounting in its domain.

### What Our Program Could Add

This is one of the best donors for the proposed escape ledger. It shows what rigorous accounting looks like:

```text
class: asynchronous deterministic distributed systems with crash fault
claim killed: guaranteed consensus termination
escape: synchrony, randomization, failure detectors, weakened semantics, coordination
residue: safety/liveness tradeoff frontier
```

### Research Implication

Use distributed systems as the gold-standard model of class-relative escape accounting.

## Terrain Cluster 6: Resilience, Panarchy, Regime Shifts, And Critical Transitions

### Core Work

Ecological resilience and social-ecological systems distinguish stability, resilience, adaptability, transformability, basins, thresholds, and cross-scale adaptive cycles.

Representative sources:

### What It Already Owns

- Collapse as basin shift.
- Resilience as persistence within a regime.
- Transformability as capacity to create a new regime when the old one is untenable.
- Critical slowing down and early-warning indicators.
- Cross-scale "remember" and "revolt" effects in panarchy.

### Generalizable Primitive

`Basin + threshold + adaptive capacity + transformability`

### Absorber Risk For Our Program

Resilience theory can absorb many collapse-prevention concepts, especially in ecological/governance domains.

### What Our Program Could Add

Resilience theory often asks whether a system remains within or transforms regimes. The proposed accounting asks:

- what completion or absorber defines the regime?
- what local-to-global obstruction appears before transition?
- what imported structure enables transformability?
- does transformation preserve productive capacity or merely move to a new basin?

### Research Implication

Resilience provides collapse-margin language, but not by itself a no-go/escape ledger.

### Research Implication

## Terrain Cluster 8: Markets, Measures, Reflexivity, And Informational Collapse

### Core Work

Goodhart's law, Campbell's law, the Lucas critique, and Grossman-Stiglitz all describe collapse of measurement, policy, or market information when the observer, metric, or policy becomes part of the system.

Representative sources:

- Goodhart origin pointer: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goodhart%27s_law
- Campbell law origin pointer: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Campbell%27s_law
- Lucas critique: https://people.bu.edu/rking/REmodels/lucascr.pdf
- Grossman-Stiglitz, "On the Impossibility of Informationally Efficient Markets": https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=228054
- Grossman-Stiglitz PDF mirror: https://terpconnect.umd.edu/~wermers/ftpsite/FAME/grossman_stiglitz_%281980%29.pdf

### What It Already Owns

- Metric-target collapse.
- Policy changes breaking historical invariants.
- Reflexive observer effects.
- Informational efficiency impossibility when information is costly.

### Generalizable Primitive

`Readout becomes control input`

Collapse happens when an indicator, policy relation, or price signal is treated as invariant after agents adapt to it.

### Absorber Risk For Our Program

Goodhart/Campbell/Lucas could absorb metric-collapse and observer-side bookkeeping collapse.

### What Our Program Could Add

The proposed accounting can make this cross-domain:

```text
readout map R becomes target/admissibility rule A
agents adapt to R
old invariant breaks
collapse: metric no longer tracks source process
escape: rotate metrics, preserve hidden validation source, use plural observers, restore structural model
```

### Research Implication

Markets and metrics are critical for distinguishing representation growth from source growth.

### Generalizable Primitive

`Adaptive regulation with load debt`

### Absorber Risk For Our Program

### Generalizable Primitive

`Construction cost + reproduction abundance`

### Absorber Risk For Our Program

Assembly theory could absorb claims about measurable emergence from construction history, especially in chemistry/origin-of-life contexts.

### Research Implication

### Generalizable Primitive

`Closure that enables continued self-maintenance`

### Absorber Risk For Our Program

This literature can absorb "systems generate/preserve themselves" claims.

### Research Implication

This terrain sharpens a central thesis: collapse prevention cannot mean "avoid closure." It must mean "distinguish productive closure from terminal closure."

Found in FLP, CAP, CALM, invariant confluence, no-free-lunch, contextuality hierarchies, and GU-style no-go maps.

Candidate packet field:

```text
class_K + assumptions_A + killed_claim_Q + escape_condition_E
```

### 3. Boundary Import

Found in model collapse, allostasis, supply chains, ecology, markets, commons, language, and AI training.

Candidate packet field:

```text
imported_structure + source_owner + failure_if_hidden
```

### 4. Residue

Found as:

- obstruction class in cohomology;
- tail distribution in model collapse;
- basin distance or critical slowing in resilience;
- diversity archive in QD;
- institutional legitimacy in commons;
- non-monotonicity/coordination need in CALM;
- costly information in Grossman-Stiglitz;

```text
residue_R + survival_under_representation_change
```

### 5. Productive Versus Terminal Obstruction

Found implicitly in:

- novelty search escaping deceptive objectives;
- ecological transformability;
- scientific anomaly;
- governance contestability;
- language drift;
- distributed systems positive frontiers.

Candidate distinction:

```text
terminal obstruction: no admissible continuation inside or outside class
productive obstruction: obstruction forces new admissibility, class growth, source growth, or boundary import
```

### 6. Closure Ambiguity

Closure is not always collapse.

Found in:

- autopoiesis;
- Rosen closure;
- commons boundaries;
- distributed consistency;
- finality;
- language standardization;
- physiology.

Candidate distinction:

```text
productive closure: enables self-maintenance and action
terminal closure: exhausts future admissibility
captured closure: preserves form while losing source legitimacy or adaptive capacity
```

### 7. Completion-Factorization

Found as a latent pattern in:

- model collapse;
- observer readout absorption;
- objective optimization traps;
- Goodhart/Campbell;
- static archives replacing living transmission;
- sheafification/globalization;
- finite-prefix novelty absorbed by whole-family completion.

Candidate test:

```text
Does the process factor through a fixed completed object plus access/projection/naming/schedule/finality maps?
```

## What Looks Most Generalizable

### Most Generalizable Formal Pattern

```text
local admissibility
  -> attempted global/class closure
  -> obstruction/no-go
  -> escape by class exit, boundary import, rule growth, or source growth
  -> residue survives or is absorbed
```

### Most Generalizable Domain Pattern

Systems collapse when an internally coherent loop loses a source of external diversity, admissibility, legitimacy, information, or intervention that it had silently depended on.

### Most Generalizable Anti-Collapse Pattern

Durable systems preserve:

- a source of variation;
- an admissibility update mechanism;
- memory/record/provenance;
- boundary imports or ecological coupling;
- reconciliation/finality;
- enough unresolved residue to prevent full completion;
- enough coherence to prevent fragmentation.

### Most Dangerous False Generalization

"All collapse is loss of openness."

The terrain rejects this. Some closure is required for life, law, consensus, physiology, markets, language, and governance. The real distinction is productive/coherent closure versus terminal/captured closure.

## Recommended Revision To North Star

Earlier candidate:

> Develop an obstruction calculus for open-ended systems: a class-relative accounting theory that tracks how local admissibility, attempted global completion, no-go obstruction, boundary import, escape move, and surviving residue transform across domains, so agents can distinguish genuine generative escape from absorbed obstruction, hidden import, observer-side novelty, premature closure, or incoherent fragmentation.

After terrain search, revise to:

> Develop a class-relative obstruction and escape accounting theory for open-ended systems, where emergence is tested by whether local admissibility survives attempted global/class closure through source growth, rule formation, boundary import, or productive residue, and collapse is classified by the way closure, completion, feedback, capture, overload, or source exhaustion absorbs that capacity.

Reason for revision:

- It no longer treats closure as automatically bad.
- It incorporates the strongest external lesson: closure can be productive, terminal, or captured.
- It does not assume a universal invariant.
- It puts "tested by" up front.

## Candidate Terrain-Informed Hypotheses

### H1: Local-to-Global Escape Hypothesis

For many domains, emergence becomes visible when locally valid behavior cannot be coherently globalized inside the current class, but the obstruction can be converted into new admissibility, rule formation, or boundary import.

### H2: Recursive Source Collapse Hypothesis

A system collapses when it replaces an independent source with recursively generated outputs while treating them as equivalent to the source.

Primary fixture: model collapse.

Secondary fixtures: language archivalization, bureaucratic metrics, synthetic markets, research citation loops.

### H3: Productive Closure Hypothesis

Open-ended systems require some closure for identity, memory, and action, but collapse occurs when closure becomes terminal or captured rather than productive.

Primary fixtures: autopoiesis, commons governance, distributed consensus, finality.

### H4: Boundary Import Burden Hypothesis

Any claimed self-contained emergence must declare which field supplies energy, diversity, admissibility, legitimacy, memory, or intervention. If the import is hidden, the emergence claim is underaccounted.

### H5: Class-Relative Positive Frontier Hypothesis

The most useful no-go results are not terminal. They identify positive frontiers like monotonicity in CALM, invariant confluence in databases, transformability in resilience, or source preservation in model-collapse mitigation.

### H6: Residue Stress-Test Hypothesis

A proposed emergence invariant must first survive the absorber map: sheafification, fixed completion, observer readout, compression, metric gaming, recursive source collapse, and basin shift.

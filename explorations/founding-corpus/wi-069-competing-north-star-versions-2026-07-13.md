# WI-069 Competing North Star Versions

Status: exploratory strategy packet
Date: 2026-07-13
Work Card: WI-069

## Purpose

Joe asked for two competing versions of the initial audacious North Star:

- the statement of what he is trying to prove;
- what agents can produce as the result;
- what type of actions the agents should take;
- the initial hypotheses worth driving down the path.

This file keeps the two versions meaningfully different.

They should not be blended too early.

## Version A: Recursive Incentive Transduction

### Short Name

**Recursive Incentive Transduction**

### Audacious North Star

> Build a cross-domain theory of recursive incentive transduction: how systems convert external sources, gradients, records, or conflicts into measurement regimes that incentivize adaptive behavior, how those behaviors reshape future incentive and measurement classes, and when the resulting feedback compounds open-ended capacity rather than collapsing into capture, exhaustion, or self-reference.

### What Joe Is Trying To Prove

Joe is trying to prove that AI-accelerated research agents can discover a real cross-domain mechanism, not just a metaphor:

> Many open-ended systems endure because they build interfaces that convert a source regime into an incentive regime, then recursively use the behavior that regime drives to create future adaptive capacity.

Examples:

```text
Bitcoin:
electricity + computation -> proof-of-work -> monetary policy -> mining/holding/infrastructure behavior -> future security/incentive ecology

Photosynthesis:
light + CO2 + water -> biochemical energy -> biomass -> trophic/ecological behavior -> future planetary/evolutionary incentive landscape
```

The deeper claim:

> Emergence is often not a new object but a recursive incentive ecology formed by a transduction interface.

### What Agents Can Get As A Result

Agents should produce:

1. A cross-domain atlas of recursive incentive transductions.
2. A `RecursiveIncentiveTransductionPacket`.
3. A typology of first-, second-, and third-order effects.
4. A collapse taxonomy for incentive regimes.
5. A positive theory of open-ended capacity compounding.
6. A set of paired contrasts that test whether the theory discriminates or merely analogizes.
7. A public-facing high-concept artifact that makes Joe's AI-accelerated thinking distinctive.

### Agent Actions

Agents should:

1. Harvest divergent examples where a source is converted into an incentive regime.
2. For each example, identify:
   - source regime;
   - transduction interface;
   - first-order target quantity;
   - first-order behavior;
   - second-order policy/ecology;
   - third-order incentive landscape;
   - feedback on the source;
   - collapse/capture modes.
3. Build paired contrasts:
   - Bitcoin vs photosynthesis;
   - bug bounties vs immune response;
   - carbon pricing vs ecological nutrient cycling;
   - open-source reputation vs academic publication;
   - model training loops vs education systems.
4. Separate productive recursive loops from self-referential collapse loops.
5. Identify the minimum formal grammar needed to avoid metaphor.
6. Pressure-test against cybernetics, evolutionary theory, economics, systems ecology, and reinforcement learning absorbers.

### Initial Hypotheses

#### A-H1: Transduction Interface Hypothesis

Open-ended systems require interfaces that convert a source regime into a target regime where the source becomes measurable as incentive, admissibility, capability, or finality.

Falsification:

Important examples of durable open-endedness do not require any identifiable transduction interface.

#### A-H2: Recursive Incentive Hypothesis

The most powerful transductions are recursive: the behaviors they incentivize modify future measurement and incentive regimes.

Falsification:

Second- and third-order effects are incidental rather than structurally necessary across examples.

#### A-H3: Source-Contact Preservation Hypothesis

Recursive incentive systems collapse when the target incentive regime loses contact with the source regime it depends on.

Examples:

- synthetic-data loops;
- financial bubbles;
- reputation farming;
- benchmark gaming.

Falsification:

Systems can remain adaptive indefinitely while measuring only internal outputs.

#### A-H4: Externality Blindness Hypothesis

Recursive incentive systems collapse or become illegitimate when the target regime rewards behavior while failing to measure source-regime costs.

Examples:

- energy externalities;
- ecological depletion;
- unpaid maintenance labor;
- social trust depletion.

Falsification:

External costs do not predict collapse, capture, or legitimacy loss.

#### A-H5: Capacity-Compounding Hypothesis

Open-ended transductions are distinguished by whether second-order behavior replenishes or expands future source capacity rather than merely consuming it.

Falsification:

Compounding and depletion cannot be distinguished except after the fact.

### Best Use

Use Version A when Joe wants the boldest, most memorable, most distinctive North Star.

### Risk

It is easier to overclaim. The examples are vivid, but the theory needs a disciplined packet protocol or it will sound like systems-poetry.

## Version B: Class-Relative Measurement / Escape Ledger

### Short Name

**Class-Relative Measurement Escape Ledger**

### Audacious North Star

> Build a class-relative accounting theory for measurement-regime transitions: how systems move from one measurement/admissibility class to another, what no-go or obstruction forces the transition, what is lost or gained, what boundary imports become necessary, and what residue or invariant survives when the old class can no longer measure the system.

### What Joe Is Trying To Prove

Joe is trying to prove that AI-accelerated research agents can take divergent examples and produce rigorous cross-domain accounting:

> Agents can identify when emergence or collapse is actually a transition to a different class-relative measurement set, then record what the old class ruled out, what the new class measures, what is imported, and what remains invariant or lost.

The deeper claim:

> Open-ended systems cannot be evaluated inside one fixed measurement class; the important scientific object is the disciplined transition between classes.

### What Agents Can Get As A Result

Agents should produce:

1. A reusable `MeasurementTransitionPacket`.
2. A `NoGoEscapeLedger`.
3. A typed list of collapse modes:
   - coarsening;
   - completion;
   - metric capture;
   - source starvation;
   - finality/readout absorption;
   - legitimacy collapse;
   - fragmentation.
4. A rigorous absorber map.
5. A class-relative hypothesis portfolio.
6. A formal or semi-formal publication path.
7. A reusable method for deciding whether a proposed new repo has a real object.

### Agent Actions

Agents should:

1. Start from known rigorous domains:
   - FLP/CALM;
   - I-confluence;
   - sheaf contextuality;
   - model collapse;
   - Goodhart/Campbell/Lucas;
   - Time as Finality projection obstruction;
   - Temporal Issuance completion/factorization.
2. For each example, identify:
   - old measurement class;
   - new measurement class;
   - no-go or obstruction;
   - assumptions doing the work;
   - lost measurements;
   - gained measurements;
   - boundary imports;
   - residue;
   - factorization status.
3. Test whether transition packets compose.
4. Kill weak examples that only work metaphorically.
5. Build a small theorem-oriented fixture before claiming a general theory.

### Initial Hypotheses

#### B-H1: Measurement-Regime Transition Hypothesis

Many apparent cases of emergence or collapse are better classified as transitions between class-relative measurement regimes.

Falsification:

The transition framing adds no discriminatory power beyond ordinary domain descriptions.

#### B-H2: Completion-Factorization Collapse Hypothesis

A process collapses relative to class `K` when its apparent novelty factors through a fixed completed object plus access, naming, projection, schedule, finality, or readout maps.

Falsification:

The relevant completion class cannot be specified non-arbitrarily.

#### B-H3: No-Go / Escape Ledger Hypothesis

Class-relative no-goes can be represented by ledger entries that preserve killed assumptions, escape moves, imports, residues, and iteration burdens.

Falsification:

Every no-go/escape account is bespoke and non-composable.

#### B-H4: Boundary Import Burden Hypothesis

Measurement-regime transitions are invalid or underaccounted when they hide the external source, legitimacy, data, energy, or intervention required for the new measurement class.

Falsification:

Boundary imports are too universal to discriminate anything.

#### B-H5: Productive / Terminal / Captured Closure Hypothesis

Closure is not collapse by default. Measurement transitions must distinguish productive closure, terminal closure, and captured closure.

Falsification:

The typology cannot classify examples more cleanly than existing domain language.

### Best Use

Use Version B when Joe wants the most publishable, technically disciplined, skeptic-resistant route.

### Risk

It may be less emotionally compelling and less distinctive. It can become a careful taxonomy rather than an audacious theory.

## Head-To-Head Comparison

| Axis | Version A: Recursive Incentive Transduction | Version B: Measurement Escape Ledger |
|---|---|---|
| Novelty | Higher | High but more adjacent to existing formal work |
| Profundity | Very high | Very high |
| Usefulness | High for broad systems diagnosis | Very high for research discipline |
| Publishability | Medium-high if narrowed | Highest |
| Disruption Joe credibility | Highest public hook | Highest rigor signal |
| Formal tractability | Medium | High |
| Risk of metaphor | Higher | Lower |
| Risk of boring taxonomy | Lower | Higher |
| Best first examples | Bitcoin/photosynthesis, markets, immune system, open source | FLP/CALM, sheaf contextuality, model collapse, Goodhart, TI/TAF/GU |
| Best proof target | Recursive loop classifier and collapse modes | Ledger composition / factorization / transition packet theorem |

## Recommended Positioning

The two versions should compete like this:

### Public / Disruption Joe North Star

Lead with Version A:

> Recursive incentive transduction.

Reason:

It is memorable, distinctive, and best aligned with Joe's AI-accelerated-thinking brand.

### Research Discipline / Publication Spine

Anchor with Version B:

> Class-relative measurement escape ledger.

Reason:

It is stricter, more publishable, and better at preventing the audacious frame from becoming metaphor.

## Two Possible Opening Statements

### Version A Opening Statement

> I am testing whether AI agents can discover a rigorous cross-domain theory of recursive incentive transduction: how systems convert external sources or gradients into incentive regimes, how those incentives drive adaptive behavior, and how the second- and third-order effects either compound future capacity or collapse into capture, exhaustion, and self-reference.

### Version B Opening Statement

> I am testing whether AI agents can build a class-relative accounting discipline for emergence and collapse: when a system changes measurement regimes, what exactly becomes measurable, what is lost, what no-go or obstruction forced the transition, what boundary imports become necessary, and what residue survives.

## Two Possible Agent Missions

### Version A Agent Mission

> Find radically divergent systems where a source regime becomes an incentive regime, then map the first-, second-, and third-order adaptive behavior it drives. Separate compounding open-ended loops from captured or exhausted loops.

### Version B Agent Mission

> Take radically divergent claims of emergence or collapse and force each through a class-relative measurement ledger. Specify the old class, new class, obstruction, escape, import, residue, and factorization status. Reject examples that only work metaphorically.

## Decision

Do not choose one permanently yet.

The next experiment should run both versions in parallel on two deliberately different pairs:

1. Version A pair:
   - Bitcoin proof-of-work vs photosynthesis.
2. Version B pair:
   - Model collapse vs language vitality.

If Version A yields disciplined second/third-order structure without metaphor, it should become the audacious North Star.

If Version B yields cleaner falsifiable packets and Version A remains too loose, Version B should become the repo spine and Version A should become the public-facing hook.

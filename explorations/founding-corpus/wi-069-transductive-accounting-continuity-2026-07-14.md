# WI-069 Transductive Accounting Continuity

Status: exploratory invariant / accounting note
Date: 2026-07-14
Work Card: WI-069

## Joe's Reframe

Joe's observation:

> There seems to be some continuity, consistency, or invariance in the accounting when these things change substrates. When the incentive for agents to find solutions changes class-relative substrates through transduction problems, it is not like the energy is gone; it is accounted for in a different material substrate. When somebody sells Bitcoin, it is not like the value is gone. It moves from being a hardened monetary policy or value sitting in the monetary instrument that attracts agentic action to the actions somebody takes, which then hopefully create value.

This adds the transport/invariance question that WI-069 has been circling:

> What survives a substrate transition?

## Caution

Do not collapse all continuity into physical conservation of energy.

There are different continuity types:

- physical conservation;
- thermodynamic degradation / free-energy loss;
- monetary accounting continuity;
- legal title continuity;
- provenance continuity;
- capability continuity;
- incentive continuity;
- legitimacy continuity;
- agency-potential continuity.

The theory should not claim that "value" is conserved like energy.

The better claim is:

> Transductive systems maintain or lose accountable continuity as quantities move across substrate, measurement, incentive, and agency regimes.

## Core Shape

```text
source substrate / quantity
  -> transduction interface
  -> target substrate / accounting regime
  -> agentic action
  -> new value / capability / residue / loss
```

The research question:

```text
What transport map makes the source quantity accountable in the target regime?
```

## Bitcoin Sale Example

### Stage 1: Monetary Instrument

Source regime:

- Bitcoin ledger;
- hardened monetary policy;
- scarcity expectation;
- settlement/security surface.

Source quantity:

- BTC balance;
- purchasing power;
- claim on liquidity;
- optionality;
- social/market value assigned to the instrument.

### Stage 2: Sale / Exchange

Transduction interface:

- exchange, counterparty, market order, custody, settlement, legal/tax context.

Transport map:

```text
BTC balance -> fiat/stablecoin/other asset purchasing power
```

What is preserved:

- purchasing power to the extent the market clears;
- legal/accounting record;
- optionality transformed into spendable capacity.

What is lost/dissipated:

- fees;
- spread;
- tax friction;
- exposure to Bitcoin's future upside;
- perhaps part of the monetary-policy orientation surface.

### Stage 3: Agentic Action

Target regime:

- human plans, spending, investment, labor, production, business formation, consumption, or donation.

Agency enabled:

- hire people;
- buy tools;
- fund research;
- build product;
- solve personal constraint;
- invest in new capability.

### Stage 4: Value Creation Or Dissipation

Possible outcome:

```text
BTC monetary value -> purchasing power -> action -> new capability/value
```

or:

```text
BTC monetary value -> purchasing power -> waste/speculation/failed action -> dissipated value
```

The continuity is not automatic. It depends on the action regime.

## Photosynthesis / Food Web Example

### Stage 1: Light

Source regime:

- solar radiation.

Source quantity:

- photons / energy flux.

### Stage 2: Photosynthetic Transduction

Transduction interface:

- pigments, electron transport, biochemical pathways.

Transport map:

```text
light energy -> chemical potential in organic molecules
```

What is preserved:

- energy transformed into chemical free energy, with losses.

What is lost/dissipated:

- heat;
- inefficiencies;
- unusable wavelengths;
- thermodynamic degradation.

### Stage 3: Biological Agency

Target regime:

- plant metabolism, growth, reproduction, ecological interactions.

Agency enabled:

- growth toward light;
- resource competition;
- reproduction;
- support of herbivores, decomposers, and food webs.

### Stage 4: Ecological Continuity

Further transport:

```text
plant biomass -> animal metabolism -> motion / reproduction / waste -> decomposer cycles -> soil fertility
```

The substrate changes repeatedly, but there is continuity of matter, energy flow, and ecological capacity.

## General Insight

Open-ended systems may depend on **accountable substrate transitions**:

```text
energy -> chemical capacity
chemical capacity -> biological agency
biological agency -> ecological niche
ecological niche -> evolutionary search space
```

or:

```text
electricity -> chainwork
chainwork -> monetary policy
monetary policy -> human incentive
human incentive -> infrastructure/action
infrastructure/action -> new capability or loss
```

The important question is not simply whether something is conserved.

The important question is:

> Is there a legitimate continuity relation across the regime transition, and does it preserve agency-generating capacity?

## Continuity Types

### 1. Physical Continuity

Matter and energy are transformed subject to physical constraints.

Examples:

- photosynthesis;
- metabolism;
- manufacturing;
- battery storage.

### 2. Accounting Continuity

Claims, balances, credits, rights, obligations, or titles are transferred.

Examples:

- Bitcoin sale;
- bank transfer;
- carbon credit;
- legal title transfer;
- insurance payout.

### 3. Provenance Continuity

A record connects current state to source history.

Examples:

- supply-chain traceability;
- research citation;
- chain of custody;
- blockchain transaction history;
- dataset lineage.

### 4. Incentive Continuity

An incentive remains effective after being re-denominated into a new regime.

Examples:

- grant funding becoming research labor;
- prize money becoming engineering effort;
- token rewards becoming protocol contribution;
- market price becoming production response.

### 5. Capability Continuity

Capacity survives transition into a new substrate or agent class.

Examples:

- money becoming tools;
- food becoming work;
- education becoming skill;
- research becoming method;
- infrastructure becoming future option space.

### 6. Legitimacy Continuity

Authority, consent, or recognized validity survives a transition.

Examples:

- law converting testimony into admissible evidence;
- commons rules becoming enforceable local governance;
- peer review turning claims into accepted literature;
- protocol consensus turning blocks into settlement.

## Collapse / Leak Modes

### 1. Dissipation

The source quantity is consumed but fails to create target capacity.

Example:

Spending Bitcoin on a failed project.

### 2. Broken Provenance

The target regime cannot trace the source, so legitimacy or validity fails.

Example:

dataset without lineage; evidence without chain of custody.

### 3. Accounting Mismatch

The target unit does not actually preserve what the source unit meant.

Example:

carbon offsets that do not correspond to real emissions reductions.

### 4. Capture

The continuity relation is controlled by actors who extract value without preserving agency capacity.

Example:

platform reputation converted into rent extraction.

### 5. Externality Blindness

The continuity ledger tracks target value while ignoring source-regime cost.

Example:

monetary gains that ignore ecological damage.

### 6. Agency Failure

The transition preserves value/power but fails to create adaptive action.

Example:

capital sits idle or funds nonproductive behavior.

## Hypotheses

### TAC-H1: Continuity Ledger Hypothesis

Every strong agency-generating transduction requires an accountable continuity relation across substrate or regime transitions.

Falsification:

Strong examples of agency-generating transduction exist with no identifiable continuity relation.

### TAC-H2: Agency Potential Is Transported, Not Conserved

What matters is not strict conservation of value but transport of agency-enabling potential across regimes.

Falsification:

Agency outcomes are independent of whether transport preserves purchasing power, energy, legitimacy, provenance, or capability.

### TAC-H3: Collapse As Continuity Break

Many collapse modes are continuity failures:

- source quantity is dissipated;
- provenance breaks;
- target accounting mismatches source reality;
- externalities are excluded;
- agency is not generated.

Falsification:

Collapse cases do not correlate with broken continuity, accounting mismatch, or dissipation.

### TAC-H4: Compounding Requires Continuity Plus Productive Agency

A transduction compounds capacity only when the target regime both preserves enough continuity and enables productive agency.

Falsification:

Continuity alone is enough, or productive agency alone is enough, across examples.

## Methodology Update

Every future packet should add:

```text
Continuity:
  What source quantity crosses regimes?

Transport:
  What map or institution makes the crossing valid?

Loss:
  What is dissipated, taxed, spent, degraded, or externalized?

Agency:
  What new action does the target quantity enable?

Trace:
  Can the system account for the transition afterward?

Compounding:
  Does the action replenish or expand future capacity?
```

## Current Best North Star With Continuity Included

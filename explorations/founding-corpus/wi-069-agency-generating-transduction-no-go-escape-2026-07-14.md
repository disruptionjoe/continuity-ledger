Status: exploratory North Star refinement

## Joe's Reframe

Joe's observation:

> There seems to be something really fundamental in the evolutionary attempt for this transduction to find new ways of creating agency that can find new solutions around class-relative no-go.

This is likely the strongest formulation so far.

## Core Shape

The object is not just:

```text
substrate -> transduction -> incentive -> behavior
```

It is:

```text
substrate / gradient / constraint
  -> transduction interface
  -> predictability / policy / incentive surface
  -> new agency class
  -> search for solutions outside the old class-relative no-go
  -> new transductions, agencies, and measurement regimes
```

## Central Hypothesis

> Open-ended systems endure by evolving or constructing transduction interfaces that turn available substrates into new agency classes. Those agency classes can search solution spaces that were inaccessible, inadmissible, or impossible inside the prior class. Collapse occurs when the system can no longer generate, preserve, or legitimate new agency around its class-relative no-goes.

## Why This Is Deeper Than The Prior Frame

Prior frame:

> A system converts substrate into a predictability surface that incentivizes adaptive behavior.

Deeper frame:

> A system converts substrate into an agency-generating surface, and the generated agency searches around the no-go constraints of the prior system class.

The key new object is:

```text
agency generation around class-relative no-go
```

## Examples

- light, CO2, water, biochemical machinery.

Transduction:

- light becomes chemical energy and biomass.

Agency generated:

- plants gain new growth capacity;
- ecological niches emerge;
- food webs become possible;
- oxygenation supports new metabolic agency classes.

Class-relative escape:

- organisms are no longer limited to prior chemical energy access patterns;
- light becomes a source for expanded biological search and ecological construction.

- trophic ecosystems;
- atmospheric transformation;
- evolutionary expansion of aerobic life.

## Key Terms

### Agency Class

A class of actors, processes, organisms, institutions, agents, or subsystems capable of taking adaptive action under a policy/predictability surface.

### Agency-Generating Surface

A surface that does not merely reward existing agents but helps create, recruit, differentiate, or sustain new agents.

Examples:

- Bitcoin monetary policy creates miners and infrastructure builders.
- Photosynthesis creates plant and trophic agency.
- Market prices create entrepreneurs and arbitrageurs.
- Peer review and prizes create research communities.

### Class-Relative No-Go

A constraint or impossibility inside a prior class.

The new agency class does not necessarily refute the no-go. It may route around it by changing class, substrate, interface, or measurement regime.

## Strongest North Star Candidate

> Develop a theory of agency-generating transduction: how open-ended systems convert substrates, gradients, constraints, and no-go pressures into policy or predictability surfaces that create new agency classes capable of searching beyond prior class-relative limits; and classify when these recursive agency ecologies compound capacity versus collapse into capture, exhaustion, self-reference, or illegitimate closure.

## What Agents Are Proving

Agents are not proving that every system is hypercomputational.

They are testing whether:

> Cross-domain AI research can identify a recurrent mechanism by which systems escape fixed-class limitations: they transduce substrate into agency-generating surfaces, then those agencies search and construct outside the old measurement/no-go class.

## Initial Hypotheses

### AGT-H1: Agency-Generating Transduction Hypothesis

Durable open-ended systems contain or discover transduction interfaces that create new agency classes, not merely new outputs.

Falsification:

Strong open-ended systems can be explained without generated or recruited agency classes.

### AGT-H2: Class-Relative Escape By Agency Hypothesis

Generated agency classes can search around no-goes that bind the prior class without violating the no-go itself.

Falsification:

Apparent escapes either violate no-go assumptions illegitimately or reduce to ordinary optimization inside the old class.

### AGT-H3: Predictability Surface Hypothesis

Agency generation requires a stable enough predictability, policy, reward, or orientation surface for agents to adapt around.

Falsification:

New agency classes reliably form without any stable surface of expectation or action.

### AGT-H4: Recursive Ecology Hypothesis

The second- and third-order effects of generated agency reshape the future measurement/incentive regime.

Falsification:

Downstream agency behavior does not feed back into future conditions except incidentally.

### AGT-H5: Collapse By Agency Exhaustion Hypothesis

Collapse occurs when the system can no longer generate, recruit, maintain, or legitimate agency capable of searching outside its current class-relative constraints.

Falsification:

Systems collapse or endure independently of agency generation/maintenance.

# Photosynthesis Agency-Surface Dossier

## Source Boundary

This draft dossier covers the oxygenic photosynthesis agency-surface queue item
for `cl001-oxygenic-photosynthesis`. It is scoped to primary production,
standing biomass, trophic/ecological support, and biogeochemical feedback as
candidate evidence for `P`, `A`, `Omega`, `F`, `Z`, and `V`.

Sources used:

- Christopher B. Field, Michael J. Behrenfeld, James T. Randerson, and Paul
  Falkowski, "Primary Production of the Biosphere: Integrating Terrestrial and
  Oceanic Components", Science 281, 237-240 (1998),
  `https://doi.org/10.1126/science.281.5374.237`.
- Paul G. Falkowski, Richard T. Barber, and Victor Smetacek, "Biogeochemical
  Controls and Feedbacks on Ocean Primary Production", Science 281, 200-206
  (1998), `https://doi.org/10.1126/science.281.5374.200`.
- Raymond L. Lindeman, "The Trophic-Dynamic Aspect of Ecology", Ecology 23,
  399-417 (1942), `https://doi.org/10.2307/1930126`.
- Yinon M. Bar-On, Rob Phillips, and Ron Milo, "The biomass distribution on
  Earth", Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences 115, 6506-6511
  (2018), `https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1711842115`.

The Field et al. and Bar-On et al. papers are quantitative synthesis sources for
primary production and standing biomass. Falkowski et al. is a source for ocean
primary-production controls and feedbacks. Lindeman is a primary ecology source
for trophic-dynamic framing. Together they can orient photosynthesis
agency-surface packet work, but they do not populate packet fields, do not score
any gate, do not prove agency generation, and do not establish that oxygenic
photosynthesis differs from the solar-heating null for a non-definitional
reason.

This dossier does not replace a biochemical carbon-fixation source, an
organism-specific growth source, or a later packet-field review.

### What This Source Does Not Establish

This dossier does not populate the oxygenic photosynthesis packet fields. It
does not score any gate, issue a CL-001 verdict, accept a repo-owned claim, or
establish that the photosynthesis target differs from the solar-heating
inert-matter null for a non-definitional reason.

It does not establish:

- a complete carbon-fixation mechanism or Calvin-cycle source pass;
- organism-specific growth, maintenance, reproduction, or behavior;
- that ecological dependence equals generated agency;
- a class-relative no-go or constructive escape witness;
- a full heat, respiration, decomposition, nutrient, or environmental loss
  budget;
- that photosynthesis should pass any CL-001 gate; or
- that primary production, biomass, trophic support, and biogeochemical
  feedback are interchangeable measures of one conserved quantity.
